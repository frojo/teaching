Lighting overview

Types of lights

  • Point Light, a Light that’s located at a point in the Scene and emits light in all directions equally
  • Spot Light, a Light that’s located at a point in the Scene and emits light in a cone shape
  • Directional Light, a Light that’s located infinitely far away and emits light in one direction only
  • Area Light, a Light that’s defined by a rectangle or disc in the Scene, and emits light in all directions uniformly across its surface area but only from one side of the rectangle or disc

Directional and Points lights are most common

The Lighting Window

The Lighting window (menu: Window > Rendering > Lighting) is the main control point for Unity’s lighting features. You can use the Lighting window to adjust settings related to the lighting in your Scene, and to optimise your precomputed lighting data for quality, bake time, and storage space.

Global Illumination

Baked v Realtime

Light Modes

Baked: Unity pre-calculates the illumination from Baked Lights before runtime (aka playing the game)

Realtime: Unity calculates and updates the lighting of Realtime Lights every frame at runtime. Unity does not precompute any calculations for Realtime Lights.

Mixed: Unity performs some calculations for Mixed Lights in advance, and some calculations at runtime. For more information, see Mixed Lights

How to Bake your lighting

  1. Select your 3D objects and turn on Contribute Global Illumination

  1. Select your lights and change their Mode from Realtime to Baked

  1. In your Lighting window in the Scene tab, click Generate Lighting (at the bottom)
