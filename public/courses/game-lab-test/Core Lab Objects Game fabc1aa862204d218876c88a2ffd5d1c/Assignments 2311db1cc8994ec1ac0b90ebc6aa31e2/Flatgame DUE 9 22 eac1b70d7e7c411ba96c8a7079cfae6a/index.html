Flatgame | DUE 9/22

▶️ IN UNITY: This week, create a *flatgame.*

Optional: Read this chapter by Lee Tussman in Interactive Storytelling for the Screen to get a sense of what a flatgame is, its history, and its surrounding context. Click here to download

To summarize, flatgames:

  • are inspired by or recreate real events and places
  • have art made physically in less than an hour
  • have no sound effects - only a single soundtrack
  • have no mechanics (including collisions) except movement and animation

Flatgames are great for:

  • practicing how to make games quickly (a vital skill)
  • composing 2D spaces/worlds that will be explored by a player
  • thinking about the nature of interactivity

Rules

You do not have to be completely faithful to all the flatgame “rules” above. But aim to keep your work in the spirit of flatgames.

Some tips, tricks and guidelines:

  • Work in 2D Mode!!
    • To put your Unity in 2D mode, click on 2D in the Scene view
    • Set your Main Camera to Orthogonal (not Perspective)
  • Create quickly and without too much thought. Games require a lot of assets, so try not to spend much time on each asset.
  • Keep it flat, both visually and mechanically. Don’t add additional functionality or interaction.
  • If you want to use pixel art, set the Filter Mode to Point if you don’t want it to be blurry

Software to create assets:

  • JS Paint: https://paint.js.org/

  • Krita: https://krita.org/

  • Heavypaint: https://www.heavypaint.com/

  • Synfig: https://www.synfig.org/

  • Pixelorama: https://orama-interactive.itch.io/pixelorama

  • Use your assets and animate them in Unity, using animator components and controller scripts.

  • build out to WebGL, upload to itch.io, and submit it on Canvas
